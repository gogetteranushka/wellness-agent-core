FOOD GROUP: FRUITS

Description:
Fruits are nature's nutritious packages providing vitamins, minerals, dietary fiber, and natural sugars along with beneficial plant compounds.

Common Indian Fruits:
- Mango
- Banana
- Papaya
- Guava
- Orange
- Apple
- Pomegranate
- Watermelon
- Grapes
- Pineapple
- Sapota (Chikoo)
- Custard apple
- Jamun (Indian blackberry)
- Amla (Indian gooseberry)
- Seasonal berries

Nutritional Benefits:
- Excellent source of vitamin C (citrus fruits, guava, amla)
- Rich in vitamin A precursors (mango, papaya)
- Good source of potassium (banana)
- Provide dietary fiber for digestive health
- Contain natural antioxidants and phytochemicals
- Natural source of simple carbohydrates for quick energy

Health Benefits:
- Support immune function
- Promote healthy digestion and prevent constipation
- Help maintain healthy blood pressure
- Reduce risk of chronic diseases
- Support skin and eye health
- Provide hydration, especially water-rich fruits

Recommendations:
- Consume at least 100-200 grams of fruits daily
- Prefer whole fruits over fruit juices
- Choose locally available, seasonal fruits
- Include variety of colors for diverse nutrients
- Eat fruits with skin when edible for added fiber
- Distribute fruit intake throughout the day

Important Notes:
- Whole fruits are preferred over juices as they retain fiber
- Diabetic individuals should monitor portion sizes and choose low-GI fruits
- Fruits can be consumed as snacks between meals
- Fresh fruits are preferred over canned or processed options
- Wash fruits thoroughly before consumption

Best Practices:
- Eat fruits when ripe for optimal nutrient content
- Store properly to maintain freshness and nutrient value
- Avoid adding sugar or salt to fruits
- Include at least one citrus fruit daily for vitamin C
