FOOD GROUP: NUTS, OILSEEDS, OILS AND FATS

Description:
Nuts, oilseeds, and oils provide essential fatty acids, fat-soluble vitamins, and energy. While nutritionally important, they should be consumed in moderation due to high calorie content.

Nuts and Oilseeds:
- Almonds (Badam)
- Cashews (Kaju)
- Walnuts (Akhrot)
- Pistachios (Pista)
- Peanuts (Moongphali)
- Sesame seeds (Til)
- Flaxseeds (Alsi)
- Sunflower seeds
- Pumpkin seeds
- Coconut

Cooking Oils:
- Groundnut oil
- Mustard oil
- Sesame oil
- Rice bran oil
- Sunflower oil
- Safflower oil
- Soybean oil
- Olive oil
- Coconut oil
- Ghee (clarified butter)

Nutritional Benefits:
- Rich source of essential fatty acids (omega-3 and omega-6)
- Provide vitamin E, a powerful antioxidant
- Good source of protein and dietary fiber
- Contain minerals: magnesium, selenium, zinc
- Provide plant sterols that support heart health

Health Benefits of Nuts and Seeds:
- Support cardiovascular health
- Help manage blood sugar levels
- Provide satiety and support weight management when consumed in moderation
- Rich in antioxidants protecting against oxidative stress
- Support brain health (omega-3 from walnuts, flaxseeds)

Oil Recommendations:
- Use a combination of different oils for balanced fatty acid profile
- Total visible fat intake should not exceed 30% of total energy
- Preferably use cold-pressed or minimally processed oils
- Rotate between different oil types (mustard, groundnut, sesame, rice bran)
- Each oil has unique fatty acid composition; variety is beneficial

Fats to Limit or Avoid:
- Trans fats (partially hydrogenated oils, vanaspati)
- Excessive saturated fats
- Reused/reheated cooking oils
- Deep-fried foods

Recommendations:
- Consume a small handful of nuts daily (20-30 grams)
- Use 4-5 teaspoons of oil per person per day for cooking
- Include ground flaxseeds or walnuts for omega-3 fatty acids
- Avoid deep frying; prefer shallow frying, saut√©ing, or steaming
- Store oils in cool, dark places to prevent rancidity
- Do not reuse cooking oil

Cooking Tips:
- Use minimal oil for cooking
- Prefer non-stick cookware to reduce oil use
- Use oil sprays or brushing instead of pouring
- Avoid heating oils to smoking point
- Add nuts to meals for nutrition and texture

Important Notes:
- Nuts and oilseeds should contribute approximately 8-10% of daily energy intake
- High calorie content requires portion control
- Roasted nuts preferred over fried or salted varieties
- Coconut oil is high in saturated fat; use in moderation
