FOOD GROUP: VEGETABLES

Description:
Vegetables are essential components of a balanced diet, providing vitamins, minerals, dietary fiber, and protective phytochemicals with minimal calories.

Categories of Vegetables:

1. Green Leafy Vegetables (GLVs):
- Spinach (Palak)
- Fenugreek leaves (Methi)
- Amaranth leaves (Chaulai)
- Mustard greens (Sarson)
- Colocasia leaves (Arbi ke patte)
- Drumstick leaves (Moringa)

2. Other Vegetables:
- Tomatoes
- Brinjal/Eggplant
- Okra/Ladies finger (Bhindi)
- Bottle gourd (Lauki)
- Ridge gourd (Turai)
- Bitter gourd (Karela)
- Cauliflower
- Cabbage
- Beans
- Carrots
- Bell peppers/Capsicum
- Pumpkin
- Drumsticks

Nutritional Benefits:
- Rich source of vitamins A, C, K, and folate
- Excellent source of minerals: iron, calcium, potassium, magnesium
- High dietary fiber content aids digestion
- Low in calories, suitable for weight management
- Contain antioxidants and phytochemicals

Special Importance of Green Leafy Vegetables:
- Exceptionally rich in iron, calcium, and beta-carotene
- Cost-effective source of micronutrients
- Help prevent anemia and support bone health
- Should be consumed daily, especially by women and children

Health Benefits:
- Reduce risk of chronic diseases (heart disease, diabetes, certain cancers)
- Support healthy digestion and prevent constipation
- Maintain healthy blood pressure
- Support eye health and immunity
- Aid in maintaining healthy skin

Recommendations:
- Consume at least 300-400 grams of vegetables daily
- Include green leafy vegetables daily (100 grams minimum)
- Choose variety of colors for diverse nutrients
- Prefer locally grown, seasonal vegetables
- Minimize cooking time to preserve nutrients
- Use minimal oil in preparation

Cooking Tips:
- Steam, stir-fry, or lightly saut√© to retain nutrients
- Use cooking water in preparations to retain water-soluble vitamins
- Add lemon juice to enhance iron absorption from GLVs
- Avoid overcooking which destroys heat-sensitive vitamins
