MACRONUTRIENT GUIDELINES

CARBOHYDRATES:

Recommended Intake:
- Should provide less than 45% of total daily energy intake
- Emphasis on complex carbohydrates over simple sugars

Types of Carbohydrates:

1. Simple Carbohydrates:
- Quick energy source from sugars
- Found in fruits, milk, table sugar, sweets
- Limit added sugars and refined products

2. Complex Carbohydrates:
- Slow-release energy from starches
- Found in whole grains, millets, pulses, vegetables
- Preferred source for sustained energy

Dietary Fiber:
- Minimum 40 grams per day recommended
- Found in whole grains, fruits, vegetables, pulses
- Promotes digestive health and disease prevention

Recommendations:
- Choose whole grains over refined grains
- Include millets and traditional grains
- Limit intake of refined flour (maida) products
- Avoid excessive consumption of sweets and sugary beverages
- Include adequate fiber through plant foods

---

PROTEINS:

Recommended Intake:
- Should provide approximately 14-15% of total daily energy intake
- Emphasis on diverse protein sources

Protein Quality:
- Complete proteins contain all essential amino acids
- Animal proteins are complete proteins
- Plant proteins should be combined for amino acid complementation

Protein Sources:

1. Plant Sources:
- Pulses and legumes
- Nuts and seeds
- Whole grains and millets
- Soybean and soy products

2. Animal Sources:
- Milk and milk products
- Eggs
- Fish and seafood
- Poultry and meat

Recommendations:
- Include protein in every meal
- Combine cereals with pulses for complete protein
- Distribute protein intake throughout the day
- Prefer plant proteins; supplement with dairy and eggs
- Choose lean cuts of meat and fish

---

FATS:

Recommended Intake:
- Total fat should not exceed 30% of total daily energy intake
- Visible fats (cooking oils, ghee, butter) should be limited to 20 grams per day

Types of Fats:

1. Saturated Fats:
- Found in animal fats, coconut oil, palm oil
- Should be limited (less than 10% of energy)
- Excessive intake increases cardiovascular disease risk

2. Unsaturated Fats (Healthy Fats):
- Monounsaturated: olive oil, mustard oil, groundnut oil
- Polyunsaturated: sunflower oil, safflower oil, fish oil
- Include omega-3 and omega-6 essential fatty acids

3. Trans Fats:
- Found in partially hydrogenated oils, vanaspati, processed foods
- Should be completely avoided
- Highly detrimental to cardiovascular health

Recommendations:
- Use a variety of cooking oils
- Limit total visible fat intake
- Completely avoid trans fats
- Include sources of omega-3 fatty acids (fish, flaxseeds, walnuts)
- Minimize deep frying
- Choose cooking methods that require less oil
- Read food labels to identify hidden trans fats

---

WATER:

Recommended Intake:
- Adults: Minimum 2-3 liters (8-10 glasses) per day
- Higher in hot climates and with physical activity
- Individual needs vary based on activity, climate, and health status

Functions of Water:
- Essential for all body functions
- Regulates body temperature
- Transports nutrients and oxygen
- Removes waste products
- Lubricates joints
- Maintains blood volume

Recommendations:
- Drink water regularly throughout the day
- Don't wait for thirst; maintain hydration proactively
- Increase intake during hot weather and physical activity
- Prefer plain water over sweetened beverages
- Include water-rich fruits and vegetables
- Monitor urine color (pale yellow indicates good hydration)

Sources of Hydration:
- Plain water (primary source)
- Buttermilk, coconut water
- Soups and broths
- Herbal teas (unsweetened)
- Fruits and vegetables with high water content
