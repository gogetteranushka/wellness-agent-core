DIET FOR PREVENTION AND MANAGEMENT OF DIABETES

Introduction:
Type 2 diabetes mellitus is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by high blood glucose levels. Dietary management is fundamental to preventing diabetes in at-risk individuals and managing the condition in those diagnosed.

Risk Factors for Diabetes:
- Family history of diabetes
- Overweight and obesity (especially abdominal obesity)
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Unhealthy diet high in refined carbohydrates and sugars
- Age above 40 years (risk increases with age)
- History of gestational diabetes
- Metabolic syndrome

Dietary Goals for Diabetes Prevention and Management:

1. Achieve and Maintain Healthy Body Weight:
- BMI 18.5-22.9 kg/mÂ² (Asian standards)
- Even 5-10% weight loss significantly improves blood sugar control
- Focus on gradual, sustainable weight loss

2. Control Blood Glucose Levels:
- Aim for normal or near-normal blood glucose
- Prevent dangerous highs and lows
- Reduce HbA1c to target levels

3. Manage Lipid Profile:
- Control cholesterol and triglycerides
- Reduce cardiovascular disease risk

4. Maintain Healthy Blood Pressure:
- Target below 140/90 mmHg
- Diet plays crucial role in blood pressure control

Key Dietary Recommendations:

CARBOHYDRATES:

1. Choose Low Glycemic Index (GI) Foods:
- Prefer foods that release glucose slowly into bloodstream
- Low GI options: whole grains, millets (ragi, jowar, bajra), pulses, non-starchy vegetables
- Avoid high GI foods: white rice, refined flour (maida), sugar, sweets

2. Control Portion Sizes:
- Carbohydrates should provide less than 45% of total energy
- Distribute carbohydrate intake evenly across meals
- Avoid large quantities of carbohydrates in single meal

3. Emphasize Dietary Fiber:
- Minimum 40 grams of fiber daily
- Helps slow glucose absorption
- Improves satiety and weight management
- Sources: whole grains, vegetables, fruits with skin, pulses

4. Limit Simple Sugars:
- Avoid table sugar, honey, jaggery
- Eliminate sugar-sweetened beverages
- Limit sweets and desserts
- Avoid fruit juices; choose whole fruits instead

PROTEINS:

- Include adequate protein (14-15% of energy)
- Helps maintain muscle mass during weight loss
- Provides satiety
- Choose: pulses, legumes, low-fat dairy, eggs, fish, lean poultry
- Limit red meat

FATS:

- Keep total fat below 30% of energy
- Choose healthy fats: mustard oil, olive oil, nuts in moderation
- Limit saturated fats: ghee, butter, coconut oil
- Completely avoid trans fats
- Include omega-3 sources: fish, flaxseeds, walnuts

SPECIFIC FOOD RECOMMENDATIONS:

Cereals and Millets:
- Prefer whole grains over polished/refined
- Choose millets: ragi, jowar, bajra (low GI, high fiber)
- Include brown rice, whole wheat
- Limit white rice and refined flour products
- Avoid bakery products made with maida

Pulses and Legumes:
- Excellent choice for diabetics (low GI, high protein, high fiber)
- Include daily in at least one meal
- Good options: moong dal, chana, rajma, kulthi
- Can be included liberally

Vegetables:
- Consume liberally (at least 400 grams daily)
- Emphasize non-starchy vegetables
- Include green leafy vegetables daily
- Good options: greens, cauliflower, cabbage, beans, okra, bitter gourd, bottle gourd, ridge gourd
- Limit starchy vegetables: potatoes, sweet potatoes, yam (control portions)

Fruits:
- Choose fruits with lower GI and higher fiber
- Good choices: guava, papaya, apple, pear, orange, sweet lime, berries, jamun
- Limit high-sugar fruits: mango, grapes, banana, chikoo
- Consume whole fruits with skin (not juices)
- Control portion sizes (one small fruit at a time)
- Distribute fruit intake throughout day

Milk and Dairy:
- Choose low-fat or skimmed options
- Limit full-fat dairy
- Include curd (unsweetened)
- Moderate portions of paneer and cheese

Nuts and Seeds:
- Small portions (handful) provide healthy fats and protein
- Good for blood sugar control
- Choose almonds, walnuts, flaxseeds
- Avoid sweetened or salted varieties

Foods to Avoid:
- Sugar and sweets (mithai, chocolates, candies)
- Sugar-sweetened beverages (soft drinks, packaged juices, sweetened tea/coffee)
- Refined carbohydrates (maida products, white bread, biscuits, cakes, pastries)
- Fried and processed snacks (chips, namkeen, samosas, pakoras)
- Fast food and junk food
- Processed meats
- Full-fat dairy products
