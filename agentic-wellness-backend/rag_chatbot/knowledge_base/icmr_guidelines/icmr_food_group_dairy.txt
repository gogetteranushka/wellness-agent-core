FOOD GROUP: MILK AND MILK PRODUCTS

Description:
Milk and milk products are excellent sources of high-quality protein, calcium, and other essential nutrients important for bone health and overall growth.

Common Dairy Products:
- Milk (cow, buffalo, goat)
- Curd/Yogurt (Dahi)
- Buttermilk (Chaas)
- Paneer (cottage cheese)
- Cheese
- Ghee (clarified butter)
- Khoa/Mawa

Nutritional Benefits:
- High-quality complete protein with all essential amino acids
- Richest source of bioavailable calcium
- Good source of vitamin B12 and riboflavin
- Provides vitamin A and vitamin D (when fortified)
- Contains phosphorus, potassium, and magnesium
- Fermented products provide beneficial probiotics

Health Benefits:
- Essential for bone and teeth development and maintenance
- Supports muscle growth and repair
- Fermented dairy supports gut health
- Helps meet protein needs, especially for vegetarians
- Supports healthy weight management when low-fat options chosen

Recommendations:
- Include milk or milk products daily
- Adults: 300 ml milk or equivalent dairy products
- Children and adolescents: 500 ml milk or equivalent
- Pregnant and lactating women: 500 ml milk or equivalent
- Choose low-fat or skimmed options for weight management
- Prefer fermented products like curd and buttermilk for digestive health

Lactose Intolerance Considerations:
- Choose fermented dairy products (curd, buttermilk) which are better tolerated
- Consume smaller portions of milk at a time
- Consider lactose-free milk alternatives if needed
- Include other calcium-rich foods in diet

Traditional Preparations:
- Lassi
- Raita
- Kadhi
- Paneer preparations
- Milk-based sweets (in moderation)

Important Notes:
- Ghee and butter are concentrated fat sources; use in moderation
- Full-fat dairy provides more calories; choose based on individual needs
- Dairy contributes approximately 8-10% of total daily energy intake
