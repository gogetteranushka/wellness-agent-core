FOOD GROUP: ANIMAL FOODS (MEAT, FISH, POULTRY, EGGS)

Description:
Animal foods provide high-quality protein, essential fatty acids, and important micronutrients like iron, zinc, and vitamin B12 that may be limited in plant-based diets.

Types of Animal Foods:

1. Eggs:
- Excellent source of high-quality protein
- Rich in choline, vitamin B12, and vitamin D
- Affordable and versatile protein source

2. Fish and Seafood:
- Rich in omega-3 fatty acids (especially fatty fish)
- Excellent protein source with low saturated fat
- Good source of iodine, selenium, and vitamin D
- Include: pomfret, mackerel, sardines, salmon, prawns, shellfish

3. Poultry:
- Chicken and other poultry
- Lean protein source when skin is removed
- Lower in saturated fat compared to red meat

4. Meat:
- Mutton, goat, lamb
- Red meat provides high bioavailable iron and zinc
- Should be consumed in moderation

Nutritional Benefits:
- Complete protein with all essential amino acids
- Highly bioavailable iron (heme iron) preventing anemia
- Excellent source of vitamin B12 (found only in animal foods)
- Rich in zinc supporting immunity and growth
- Provides omega-3 fatty acids (fish)
- Good source of selenium and other trace minerals

Health Considerations:
- Fish consumption associated with reduced cardiovascular disease risk
- Eggs are nutrient-dense with minimal impact on blood cholesterol for most people
- Red meat should be limited due to saturated fat content
- Processed and cured meats should be avoided or minimized

Recommendations:
- Include fish 2-3 times per week
- Eggs can be consumed daily (1-2 eggs) by healthy individuals
- Limit red meat consumption
- Choose lean cuts and remove visible fat
- Use healthy cooking methods: grilling, baking, steaming, boiling
- Avoid deep frying and excessive oil use
- Avoid processed meats like sausages, salami, bacon

Cooking Tips:
- Remove skin from poultry before or after cooking
- Avoid charring meat at high temperatures
- Use minimal oil in preparation
- Include with plenty of vegetables
- Marinate with spices and herbs for flavor without excess fat

Special Notes:
- Vegetarians can meet protein needs through plant sources and dairy
- Vitamin B12 supplementation may be needed for strict vegetarians
- Fish is particularly recommended for cardiovascular health
