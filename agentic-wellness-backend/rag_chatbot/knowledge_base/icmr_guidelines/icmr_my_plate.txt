MY PLATE FOR THE DAY - VISUAL NUTRITION GUIDE

Concept Overview:
"My Plate for the Day" is a visual representation of a balanced meal that demonstrates how different food groups should be distributed on a plate to achieve optimal nutrition.

Plate Composition:
The plate is divided to show proportional representation of food groups:

50% of Plate: Vegetables and Fruits
- Half the plate should be filled with colorful vegetables and fruits
- Emphasizes micronutrient density and dietary fiber
- Include variety of colors for diverse phytonutrients

25% of Plate: Cereals and Millets
- Whole grains and traditional millets preferred
- Provides energy and essential B vitamins
- Include brown rice, whole wheat, jowar, bajra, ragi

25% of Plate: Protein Foods
- Pulses, legumes, dairy, eggs, meat, fish
- Essential for growth, repair, and immune function
- Plant proteins should be emphasized

Additional Components:
- Small portions of nuts and seeds
- Moderate use of oils and fats for cooking
- Adequate water intake throughout the day

Eight Food Groups:
1. Cereals and millets
2. Pulses and legumes
3. Milk and milk products
4. Roots and tubers
5. Vegetables (including green leafy vegetables)
6. Fruits
7. Nuts, oilseeds, and oils
8. Animal foods (eggs, meat, fish, poultry)

Daily Recommendation:
Consume foods from at least 5-7 different food groups daily to ensure nutritional adequacy.
