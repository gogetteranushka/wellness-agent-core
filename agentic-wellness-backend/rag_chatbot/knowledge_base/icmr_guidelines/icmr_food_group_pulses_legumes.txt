FOOD GROUP: PULSES AND LEGUMES

Description:
Pulses and legumes are vital protein sources in Indian vegetarian diets, providing essential amino acids, complex carbohydrates, dietary fiber, vitamins, and minerals.

Common Indian Pulses:
- Bengal gram/Chickpea (Chana)
- Red gram/Pigeon pea (Arhar/Tur)
- Green gram/Mung bean (Moong)
- Black gram (Urad)
- Lentils (Masoor)
- Horse gram (Kulthi)
- Kidney beans (Rajma)
- Black-eyed peas (Lobia)
- Peas (Matar)

Other Legumes:
- Soybeans
- Peanuts
- Chickpeas (Kabuli chana)

Nutritional Benefits:
- Excellent source of plant-based protein (20-25% protein content)
- Rich in complex carbohydrates and dietary fiber
- Good source of B-complex vitamins, especially folate
- Provide essential minerals: iron, zinc, magnesium, potassium
- Low in fat and cholesterol-free
- Low glycemic index foods

Health Benefits:
- Support muscle growth and repair
- Aid in diabetes management due to low GI
- Promote digestive health through fiber content
- Help reduce cardiovascular disease risk
- Support healthy weight management
- Improve satiety and reduce overall calorie intake

Complementary Protein Concept:
When combined with cereals, pulses provide complete protein with all essential amino acids. Traditional Indian combinations like dal-rice, roti-dal are nutritionally optimal.

Recommendations:
- Include pulses daily in at least one meal
- Combine with cereals for protein complementation
- Use sprouted pulses for enhanced nutrient bioavailability
- Prepare using healthy cooking methods (pressure cooking, boiling)
- Avoid excessive oil in pulse preparations

Portion Guidance:
Pulses should contribute approximately 14-15% of total daily energy intake.
