FOOD GROUP: CEREALS AND MILLETS

Description:
Cereals and millets form the staple energy source in Indian diets, providing carbohydrates, proteins, B vitamins, minerals, and dietary fiber.

Common Indian Cereals:
- Rice (white, brown, parboiled)
- Wheat (whole wheat, refined flour)
- Maize (corn)

Traditional Indian Millets:
- Pearl millet (Bajra)
- Finger millet (Ragi)
- Sorghum (Jowar)
- Foxtail millet (Kangni)
- Little millet (Kutki)
- Barnyard millet (Sanwa)
- Kodo millet (Kodon)
- Proso millet (Chena)

Nutritional Benefits:
- Primary source of energy (carbohydrates)
- Good source of plant protein when combined with pulses
- Rich in B-complex vitamins (thiamine, niacin, folic acid)
- Millets provide higher mineral content than polished rice
- Whole grains and millets are excellent sources of dietary fiber

Health Advantages of Millets:
- Lower glycemic index compared to polished rice
- Gluten-free options for sensitive individuals
- Rich in antioxidants and phytochemicals
- Better suited for diabetes management
- Climate-resilient and sustainable crops

Recommendations:
- Prefer whole grains over refined grains
- Include variety of millets in weekly meal plans
- Consume minimally processed cereals
- Limit refined flour (maida) products
- Include traditional preparations like ragi porridge, jowar roti, bajra khichdi

Portion Guidance:
Cereals and millets should contribute approximately 45% or less of total daily energy intake.
