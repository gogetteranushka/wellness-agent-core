ICMR-NIN DIETARY GUIDELINES FOR INDIANS - 2024
OVERVIEW & OBJECTIVES

Purpose:
The Dietary Guidelines for Indians provide evidence-based recommendations to promote optimal health, prevent malnutrition, and reduce the risk of diet-related non-communicable diseases among the Indian population.

Target Audience:
- General Indian population across all age groups
- Healthcare professionals and nutritionists
- Policymakers and food industry stakeholders
- Educators and community health workers

Key Goals:
1. Maintenance of optimal health and well-being
2. Prevention of undernutrition and micronutrient deficiencies
3. Prevention and management of diet-related non-communicable diseases (obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular diseases, hypertension)
4. Promotion of sustainable and culturally appropriate dietary practices
5. Enhancement of food safety and quality

Core Principles:
- Emphasis on dietary diversity and balanced nutrition
- Recognition of India's cultural and regional food diversity
- Integration of traditional food wisdom with modern nutrition science
- Focus on whole foods and minimally processed ingredients
- Sustainable and environmentally conscious food choices
